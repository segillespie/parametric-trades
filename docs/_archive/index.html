Design and Analysis of Experiments for Simulation Course

About

Course Overview Presentation

This course was developed by analysts at TRAC Fort Leavenworth with assistance from the Data Science Center of Education (DSCOE) and friends and peers throughout the Army analytic community. It is intended to serve as a practical foundation in the statistical field of the design and analysis of experiments, specifically focused on supporting simulation development and analysis. It assumes basic familiarity with statistics and introduces programming in R. It is intended to be self paced, though organizations can work through it on a set schedule as desired.

Additional Resources

There are many resources available for support in statistics, simulation, and programming. Here are a few that you might not immediately find on google:

  • DSCOE
  • Naval Postgraduate School SEED Center - Note this is a highly useful resource for "data farming" - i.e., building and analyzing simulations.
  • The Air Force Institute of Technology Scientific Test & Analysis Techniques (STAT) Center of Excellence
    • - Note, this center is focused primarily on test and evaluation, but has a great wealth of DOE resources.
  • NIST/SEMATECH e-Handbook of Statistical Methods
    • - This is a useful engineering statistics handbook produced by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), particularly, for this course, their Chapter 5 - Process Improvement.
  • R for Data Science by Grolemund and Wickham
  • Bookdown has a variety of free books related to R, data visualization, and statistics.
  • Bio Stat Handbook is a nice statistics resource.
  • Statistics How To is a nice statistics resource.
